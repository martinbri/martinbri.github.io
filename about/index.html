About Me

Hi!
I am a recent PhD graduate from ISAE-SUPAERO, Toulouse, France, where I specialized in the intersection of machine learning and computational fluid dynamics (CFD). My passion lies in bridging these two disciplines to develop innovative solutions for complex fluidic challenges.

Prior to my PhD, I earned a Master’s degree in Fluid Dynamics and Energetics from Toulouse INP - ENSEEIHT (France) in 2020, where I built a strong foundation in CFD, aerodynamics, and thermics.

Future Professional Endeavors

My goal can be summed up in one sentence:
Propose innovative machine-learning-based solutions for CFD applications.

CFD has already empowered numerous industries and research teams to better understand fluid dynamics in their applications. However, many issues remain. I believe machine learning offers a paradigm shift in this area—it can help us speed up simulations, model physical phenomena, propose reduced-order models, and learn control strategies.

I am actively seeking opportunities to bring this vision to life and contribute to cutting-edge advancements in the field.

Academic Background

PhD Research (2020–2024)

My PhD at ISAE-SUPAERO focused on the development of control strategies for navigation in urban environments, aligning with the emerging vision of urban air taxis. These vehicles have to navigate in complex flows, such as wind gusts and turbulence.

Key milestones of my research:

  1. Framework Development: Creating methodologies to enable deep reinforcement learning algorithms to interact with CFD solvers.
  2. Control Strategies: Training deep reinforcement learning algorithms to mitigate aerodynamic disturbances effectively.
  3. Real-Time Adaptation: Proposing solutions for online estimation of aerodynamic disturbances using data from vehicle sensors.

Explore the details of my work in the projects section or view my publications in the publications section.
